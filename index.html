Who am I?

I am a Junior EECS & Physics student at UC Berkeley.

I went to Singapore for high school at Victoria Junior College because of a scholarship I got from Singapore Ministry of Education after graduating from middle school.

I am a huge fun of robotics and vehicle design. At Berkeley, I spend a large amount of my free time at CalSol- UC Berkeley Solar Vehicle Team, in which we design, build, test, and race 100% solar powered vehicles. I was lucky that I got to participate in the American Solar Challenge 2012 from Rochester, NY to St. Paul, MN, a 1650-mile endurance race. This race boosted my interest on CalSol and I became the Electrical Lead for the team at Fall 2012. I was then in charge of the Electrical Team of Calsol and was involved in several major decesion making process for CalSol's next generation solar car.

Power Hub | An ARM based power-efficient low voltage power management device on the solar car

  • Designed a device to manage 12V power on solar car based on LPC11C14/24 – a Cortex-M0 ARM processor from NXP.
  • Implemented LTC4151 to monitor each line’s current and voltage individually and report to the processor through I2C bus.
  • Implemented LTC1153 electronic circuit breaker for each line and let them report status back to the processor.
  • Programmed the processor to react to CAN messages and report status and alerts through CAN.
  • Schematics avaliable upon request.

SmallWorld | A Hadoop Implementation of Parallel DFS | Project for CS 61C Machine Structure

  • Implemented a parallel Deep First Search to calculate the distribution of relationship distance from a relationship graph
  • Tested on Amazon EC2 and ElasticMapReduce with test cases of 1 million Actors from the Hollywood co-star database and 5.7 million Wikipedia contributors from the Wikipedia links circa 2011.
  • Finished the 1 million nodes test in 30 minutes and 5.7 million nodes test in 50 minutes with 48 reducers on 12 machines
  • Code available at: http://goo.gl/CPHg5
  • Inspired by Frigyes Karinthy’s Six Degrees of Separation

Transistor AM Radio (Using BJTs & MOSFETs) | Final Project for EE 105 Microelectronic Devices & Circuits

  • Designed an amplifier circuit that has high input impedance, low output impedance and can drive an 8 Ω speaker
  • Designed a tunable demodulator circuit that can receive signal from local radio stations with an antenna
  • Design summary available at: http://db.tt/crfYyz7p
  • Detailed documentation: http://db.tt/rdrxutwb
